Recipes

Bulalo

The perfect company for a cool, rainy day in the Philippines is a nice hot bowl of bulalo. This tasty soup is made by slow-cooking beef shanks and bone marrow (still in the bone) in some water with fish sauce, onions, and peppercorn, and later adding in some vegetables. Especially known for this dish is the province of Batangas in the country’s Southern Luzon region.

    Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef shanks
  • 2 bunches pechay, cleaned and discard stem ends
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 medium onion, peeled and sliced
  • 8 pieces mini corn on the cob
  • 2-3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 2 beef bouillon cubes
  • 6-7 cups of water

    Instructions:

  • 1. In a large pot, place the beef shanks and cover with water.
  • 2. Add the onion and peppercorn and bring to a boil.
  • 3. Remove scum as it rises. Then lower the heat and simmer for 2 hours or until the beef is fork tender.
  • 4. Add the potatoes, corn, fish sauce and bouillon cubes. Simmer for 5-7 minutes or until potatoes are done.
  • 5. Add the pechay in the final 1 minute of cooking. Cover the pot and turn off heat.
  • 6. Enjoy!

Sisig

Served sizzling on a hot stone plate, sisig is a favorite pulutan (beer chow) among Filipinos. The meat is primarily chopped up parts of the pigs’ face — in the Philippines, no cut of the animal goes to waste. Some recipes use either mayonnaise or raw egg (to be mixed in while hot) to give it a creamier texture but the classic way is to incorporate pig’s brain into the dish.

    Ingredients

  • 1 ½ kg pork head (ear, face, tongue and cheek)
  • ¼ cup liver, broiled and cubed
  • 1 Tbsp minced garlic
  • 1 pc chopped onion
  • 2 pcs bay leaves
  • ½ cup vinegar
  • ½ tbsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 cup beef or pork stock
  • 2 pcs calamansi
  • 3 pcs siling labuyo, chopped finely
  • Salt or fish sauce to taste

    Instructions:

  • 1. Grill the pork (ear, tongue, cheeks and face), while removing remaining facial hair.
  • 2. Boil the pork’s head after grilling until it is tender.
  • 3. Debone and chop finely.
  • 4.Sauté garlic, onion.
  • 5. Add chopped pork and liver Season with vinegar, salt and pepper.
  • 6. Pour stock and bay leaves Let it boil, then let it simmer until sauce thickens Season to taste.
  • 7. Serve and enjoy!

Bistek Tagalog

Bistek Tagalog or the Filipino beef steak is a delicious blend of salty, sour, and sweet flavours. Thinly sliced beef is marinated in a mixture of mainly soy sauce and kalamansi, fried, and then topped with caramelised onions. The onions are just sweet enough to cut into and balance the strong salty and acidic tastes infused into the meat.

    Ingredients

  • 1kg beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 100 ml cooking oil
  • 2 pcs white onions, sliced in rings
  • 2-5 pcs small Calamansi, (squeeze the juice)
  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper

    Instructions:

  • 1. Marinate beef sirloin in calamansi juice, soy sauce and pepper for 30 minutes or more. The longer the better it tastes! Set aside or place it in fridge.
  • 2. After marinating, reserve marinade and separate beef into another bowl. Set aside. Heat pan using medium heat and pour cooking oil. Fry beef sirloin until it turns slightly brownish in color. Remove extra oil, so it won’t be oily.
  • 3. Pour marinade sauce and simmer for a few more minutes until beef becomes tender.
  • 4. Add onion slices and a little bit of salt and a teaspoon of sugar to add taste and turn off heat. Garnish with spring onions.
  • 5. Serve and enjoy!
